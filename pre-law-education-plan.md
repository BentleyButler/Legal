# Pre-Law Education Plan

This eight-course foundation emphasizes writing, critical reading, quantitative/logical rigor, and independent research.

1. **Advanced Composition & Rhetoric** – High-volume long-form writing with iterative revisions to cement clarity, structure, and citation discipline.
2. **Constitutional or Canadian Government Seminar** – Primary-source reading (statutes, case law, Hansard) plus analytical essays to sharpen doctrinal analysis.
3. **Philosophy of Logic / Formal Logic** – Symbolic logic and argument structure to strengthen reasoning and LSAT-style problem solving.
4. **Statistics for Social Sciences / Econometrics I** – Quantitative literacy for interpreting empirical evidence, damages models, or tax data.
5. **Intermediate Microeconomics** – Systems thinking about incentives, regulation, and market dynamics relevant to corporate/tax practice.
6. **History Research Seminar (archival paper)** – Independent research, footnoting, and synthesis of primary/secondary sources mirroring legal memo work.
7. **Advanced Literary Analysis or Critical Theory** – Intensive textual interpretation and theory application to hone critical reading depth.
8. **Directed Research / Honors Thesis Prep** – Faculty-supervised project culminating in a major paper, replicating the structure of legal research memos.
